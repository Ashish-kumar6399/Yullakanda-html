  • After breakfast we leave the hotel and begin our journey to Nubra Valley via Khardungla, one of the highest motorable roads in the world, at 1,7580 feet above sea level (Fun fact: Umling La, the highest pass in the Ladakh region, is nearly 19,300 feet high and passes through the Umlingla Top.). In the middle of the icy Nubra Valley is the settlement of Hunder, which is positioned like an oasis. The stunning dunes of silver sand are situated at a height of 4,000 meters and are surrounded by mountains covered in snow. Bactrian Camels call it home. Located along the historic Silk Road, Hunder was formerly an path for international trade. Given its proximity to the Siachen Glacier, the highest barren terrain in the world, which separates India and Pakistan, it is currently one of the most strategically important locations. We have reservations at a boutique farmstay for the night.

Route Pangong Tso - Hunder to Pangong Tso (Tso = Lake in Tibetan) travels along the River Shyok till it reaches Shyok Village, taking around 5 hours. Located at an elevation of 14,270 feet above sea level, Pangong Lake is regarded as one of the highest saltwater lakes globally. Almost lying on the The Indo-Chinese border is divided into two parts, one in China and the other in India. As the The lake's breathtaking beauty made it an ideal setting for the well seen film 3 Idiots. Actually, the well-known yellow scooter from the movie's climax is stationed at a place called 3 Idiots Point. At its widest point, the lake measures around 5 km in width and 134 km in length. Many kinds of birds, including migratory ones, use the Pangong Tso Lake as a breeding place. The bar-headed goose and Brahminy Ducks are frequently spotted in the summertime. The lake is home to fish such Kunar Snow Trout and False Osman. We are spending the night at a stunning cottage by a lake today. Those who are interested can go outside at night to witness the Milky Way and many more stars light up in the sky.
